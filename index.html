Shared Vocabulary

The building blocks of every evaluation

TermDefinitionExample
Test CaseA single prompt + expected behavior pair. The atomic unit of evaluation.“How many PTO days?” → correct policy + citation
Acceptance CriteriaWhat “good” looks like. Specific enough that two people agree on pass/fail.“Must state 15 days and cite the Handbook”
Quality SignalA named dimension of quality derived from patterns. Agent-specific, not generic.Policy Accuracy, Source Attribution, Escalation Judgment
GraderApplies criteria to a response. Deterministic (regex, length) or model-graded (LLM judge).keywordMatch("hand wash"), modelGraded("empathetic")
Eval SetA collection of test cases measuring quality dimensions. Your reusable, growing asset.20 cases across core, variations, architecture, edge
HarnessThe engine that runs test cases, applies graders, and produces a results report.npm run eval → 17/20 passed (85%)

Evals Are Bottled Human Judgment

The bridge between what humans know is good and what AI produces at scale

Human Taste

Your best SME knows what “good” looks like.

They catch nuance no automation can replicate.

This judgment is rare, expensive, and doesn't scale.

Eval Sets

Capture that judgment once. Encode it as acceptance criteria. Apply it every time.

The artifact that transfers human taste into a repeatable, scalable process.

AI Quality

Every prompt change tested. Every model upgrade validated. Every regression caught.

Confident iteration at speed no human reviewer can match.

Eval sets are your team's most valuable AI asset. They compound over time — every production incident, every edge case, every lesson learned becomes a test case that protects you forever.

Evaluation Methods in Copilot Studio

Six grader types — choose based on what you're checking

MethodTypeWhat It ChecksExampleBest For
Keyword MatchDeterministicSpecific words/phrases appear in responseMust contain “hand wash only”Policy compliance, required phrases
Compare MeaningModel-basedSemantic similarity to expected answerMeans same as “15 days PTO”Factual accuracy, flexible phrasing
Text SimilarityDeterministicString-level similarity scoreResponse >80% similar to refVerbatim compliance, legal text
General QualityModel-basedLLM judges against a rubric you defineRate empathy, clarity 1–5Tone, helpfulness, open-ended quality
Capability UseDeterministicAgent used specific tools, sources, topicsMust invoke LookupPolicyTool invocation, routing
Custom ClassificationModel-basedClassifies response into your categorieson-topic / off-topic / escalationTriage, routing validation

Key Takeaways

Start with criteria, not code.
Write what “good” looks like in plain English before touching any eval code.
Small datasets are fine. Representative ones are essential.
10 well-chosen cases beat 100 random ones. Include happy paths, edge cases, and adversarial.
AI accelerates the scaffolding, not the thinking.
Use /eval-generator for speed. But you define criteria, review cases, and interpret results.
Eval sets compound over time.
Every incident, every edge case, every lesson learned becomes a test case that protects you forever.
The full lifecycle took you 40 minutes today.
Plan → Generate → Run → Interpret. Keep that file. That's your foundation.
“The best time to write an eval is before you write the prompt. The second best time is right now.”
